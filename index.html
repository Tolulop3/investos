🤖 WHAT IS THIS SYSTEM?

InvestOS is a signal aggregator. Every morning it scans 500+ stocks, reads financial news from 15 sources, and uses a machine learning model to rank the best opportunities for your accounts. It tells you what to consider buying, how much to put in, and where to set your stop loss.

It is not a guaranteed money-making machine. No system is. What it does is improve your odds by filtering signal from noise — the same way a good research analyst would, but automated and free.

THE ONE THING THAT MATTERS MOST
The system suggests stop losses. If a position hits your stop — execute it, no exceptions. A defined 8% loss is manageable. Holding through hope turns it into a 35% loss.
🏦 HOW TO OPEN YOUR ACCOUNTS

You need two accounts. Both are tax-advantaged — gains inside them are either tax-free or tax-deferred. Use Questrade — it has free ETF purchases and is the best option for Canadians starting out.

🏠 FHSA
• First Home Savings Account
• $8,000/yr contribution limit
• Tax deduction on contributions
• Tax-free on withdrawal for home
• Conservative only — ETFs preferred
• Max 16% drawdown — hard rule
📈 TFSA
• Tax-Free Savings Account
• ~$7,000/yr new room (check yours)
• All gains completely tax-free
• Can hold stocks, ETFs, crypto ETFs
• Growth + Income + Swing
• 4 buckets — see Buckets section
QUESTRADE SETUP STEPS:
1. Go to questrade.com → Open Account
2. Select TFSA first — this is your main account
3. Complete identity verification (SIN required)
4. Fund via Interac e-Transfer from your bank
5. Once funded — open a second application for FHSA
6. Check your TFSA contribution room at cra.gc.ca/myaccount
7. Never contribute more than your available room — 1%/month penalty
💹 HOW TO PLACE YOUR FIRST TRADE

Once your Questrade account is funded, here is how to execute a pick from this dashboard:

1
Search the ticker
Type the ticker exactly as shown — ENB.TO for TSX stocks, NVDA for US stocks (no .TO suffix for US). Make sure you're in the right account (TFSA or FHSA).
2
Choose order type
Use a Limit Order, not Market Order. A limit order lets you set the maximum price you'll pay. Set it 0.5-1% above the current ask to ensure it fills. Market orders can fill at bad prices during volatile moments.
3
Enter quantity
The system tells you a dollar amount (e.g. $200). Divide by the current price to get shares. E.g. $200 ÷ $56.88 = 3.5 shares. Questrade allows fractional shares for ETFs.
4
Set your stop loss
After your buy fills — immediately set a stop-loss order at the price the system showed. In Questrade: Orders → Stop Loss → enter the stop price. This is your safety net.
5
Review before submitting
Double-check: correct ticker, correct account, correct quantity, limit price reasonable. Then submit.
💱 CURRENCY NOTE
US stocks (no .TO) require USD. Questrade converts automatically but charges ~1.5% FX spread. For large amounts, use Norbert's Gambit to convert for free — Google "Norbert's Gambit Questrade" for a step-by-step guide. For amounts under $500, the conversion fee is small and not worth the effort.
🛑 STOP LOSSES — THE MOST IMPORTANT CONCEPT

A stop loss is a pre-set price at which you automatically sell if the stock falls. It is your insurance policy. Every position must have one.

✅ WITH STOP LOSS
Buy ENB.TO at $56.88
Set stop at $53.10 (−8%)
Stock drops to $53.10
Auto-sell. Loss = $14 on $200
Move on. Capital preserved.
❌ WITHOUT STOP LOSS
Buy ENB.TO at $56.88
No stop set
"It will recover"
Holds to $38.00
Loss = $67 on $200 (−33%)
STOP LOSS BY BUCKET:
FHSA−16% hard rule — the CRA contribution limit makes this non-negotiable Floor−8% — high quality names, tight stop Model−12% — standard stop Swing−10% — defined risk, exit fast Crypto−20% — wide stop, high volatility
📋 TFSA CONTRIBUTION LIMITS

The TFSA has an annual contribution limit. In 2024 it was $7,000. Your total room is cumulative — every year you didn't contribute, that room carries forward.

⚠️ OVER-CONTRIBUTION PENALTY
If you put in more than your available room: 1% per month penalty on the excess amount. CRA takes this seriously. Always check your room first.
How to check your room:
1. Go to canada.ca/my-cra-account
2. Sign in with your bank (Sign-In Partner) or CRA user ID
3. Look for "TFSA Room" — this is your available contribution space
4. Add deposits slowly — don't max out immediately if unsure
🪣 THE BUCKET STRUCTURE — WHY IT EXISTS

Your TFSA is split into four buckets. This is not arbitrary — each bucket has a different failure mode. They don't all fail at the same time. If the ML model is completely wrong about everything, the Floor bucket still pays you dividends.

FLOOR — 50%
Dividend core. Canadian banks, pipelines, telecom. These companies pay 5-8% dividends per year regardless of what the model thinks. Even if you never look at the dashboard again, this bucket sends you income.
Examples: ENB.TO, RY.TO, T.TO, CNR.TO
MODEL PICKS — 30%
ML-assisted growth stocks. This is where the signal engine adds value. If the model fails completely for 12 months, you lose at most 30% of your TFSA — survivable. If the model works, this is where the outperformance comes from.
Examples: NVDA, SHOP.TO, CRWD, MSFT
SWING — 15%
Short-term trades, 2-8 week holds. Higher risk, strictly size-limited. Even a complete wipeout of every swing trade costs you only 15% of your TFSA. Defined risk.
Examples: PLTR, HOOD, TSLA, AMD
CRYPTO — 5%
BTC and SOL. Written off mentally before you buy. 5% of your account. If it goes to zero — you lose 5% of your TFSA. If it 10x's — you made 50% on your account. Asymmetric bet, sized appropriately.
Examples: BTC, SOL

❓ FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Q: Do I need to invest every day?
No. Only deploy when you have money to invest and the regime is not BEAR. Sitting in cash during a BEAR regime is a valid strategy.
Q: What if I can only afford $30?
That’s fine. The deployment calculator scales to any amount. At $30 you get: $15 floor, $9 model, $4.50 swing, $1.50 crypto. Every bucket is represented.
Q: What does Score mean?
A score out of 100. Combines momentum, ML probability, RS rating, dividend yield, and news sentiment. 75+ is high conviction. 55–74 is moderate. Below 55 the system generally won’t show it.
Q: What if I disagree with a pick?
Skip it. This is a suggestion engine, not an order. If you don’t understand why a stock is in the list, don’t buy it. Only buy what you understand.
Q: What happens if GitHub goes down?
The dashboard shows the last baked data. You might see yesterday’s picks. The email you received that morning is still valid. GitHub rarely goes down.
Q: Can I use this with another broker?
Yes. Wealthsimple Trade, IBKR, TD Direct — any broker with TSX and US access works. Questrade is recommended because ETF purchases are free.
Q: Is this financial advice?
No. Personal tool for educational purposes. The model makes mistakes. Always verify before investing. Never invest money you cannot afford to lose.
Q: Why does the X feeds section show offline?
The Twitter/X API now costs $100+/month. The system uses free RSS news feeds instead, which provide equivalent or better macro signal quality.
